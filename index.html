About Me


I edit movies, TV shows and promos.

New York trained Editor Mathew Ein has worked on some of the biggest and most sucessful TV shows in the reality TV, commercial, and documentary world. From the History's white-hot "Pawn Stars" to Discovery's hit "Storm Chasers." Mathew has a career that has taken him all over the post-production world. His work has been seen at the Cannes Film Festival, the Tribeca Film Festivel, and SXSW.

Mathew Ein's career started out in New York City as as a 2nd Assistant Editor on the feature film "Sons of Sakhnin," an official selection of the Tribeca Film Festival in 2007. He has worked on a variety of projects ranging from political ads during the 2006 Democratic House and Senate Races and Hillary Clinton for President 2008. Since then, he has assisted two-time Emmy winner Patrick Gambuti, Jr. and Kurt Engfher, on the feature documentary "At the Edge of the World," a feature length documentary selected for the 2008 Toronto International Film Festival.

2011 included three of the biggest projects in his career; he worked as Assistant Editor on his first feature narrative film, the upcoming "Highland Park" (starring Billy Burke, Danny Glover and Parker Posey directed by Andrew Meieran), assistant editor on the first season of "Tough Cookies" for the Food Network, and was then brought on as Junior Editor for the latest season of Discovery Channel's hit series, Storm Chasers,for it's sixth season.

2012 began as the lead editor on Wheat Thins' "Do-Minatrix" web campaign for AKQA. That year got more busy with History Channel's hit "Call of the Wildman" (Sharp Entertainment), and Nat Geo's hit "Fish Tank Kings". Another major highlight was working as an editor on Kanye West's directorial debut, "Cruel Summer", that premiered at the Cannes Film Festival. That was soon followed by a five-month stint on History Channel's international mega-hit "Pawn Stars".

Recently, Mathew made the move to Los Angeles, and is anxious to bring his experience and talent to new, exciting projects in California.
